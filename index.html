About Alex

Whenever I see something that appears to be capable of taking photos, I want to test its image quality. I feel slightly embarrassed to say that I know the scanners and the visualisers at my high school didn’t take great photos.

My favourite genres of photography are landscape and cityscape, though I also know a little bit about architecture, event, portrait and product photography.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Were your photos edited?

Yes, they were. Editing (also known as post-processing) is an important part of photography. It facilitates artistic expression and helps photographers overcome limitations of equipment.

2. Do I need an expensive camera to take beautiful photos?

Generally speaking, you don’t. While a good camera certainly helps, it’s the person behind the lens that matters more. Three of the photos on this page were taken with a second-hand compact camera costing only HK$1800 (US$230). There are also many people out there taking amazing photos with just smartphones.

3. What camera should I get if I’m really interested in photography?

I always suggest people explore within the limits of their existing equipment first, be it a phone or a point-and-shoot camera. That was how I started. If you do need a new camera, take a look at DPReview's Buying Guides.

4. May I use your photos?

Feel free to use them for non-commercial purposes (e.g. wallpapers, school projects). If you would like to use any of them for commercial purposes, please contact me for licensing.
